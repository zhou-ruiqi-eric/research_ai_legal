Mandatory U.S. law for keeping patient health data private and secure.

- U.S. federal law (1996) protecting privacy and security of Protected Health Information (PHI).
- **Applies to**: Covered Entities (providers, health plans, clearinghouses) + Business Associates.
- **Main Rules**:
	  - Privacy Rule: Controls PHI use/disclosure + patient rights.
	  - Security Rule: Safeguards for electronic PHI (ePHI).
	  - Breach Notification Rule: Report breaches.
- **Core Requirements**: Risk analysis, admin/physical/technical safeguards, policies, training, BA Agreements, patient access/amend rights.
- **Penalties**: Civil fines up to ~$2.19M per violation type per year (inflation-adjusted); criminal fines + jail possible.
- **Enforced by**: HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR).
- **Status**: Security Rule updates proposed (not yet final).
