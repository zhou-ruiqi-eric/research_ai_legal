 Required for SOX compliance by public companies using external service providers

- AICPA attestation report on controls at a service organization that are relevant to user entities’ **internal control over financial reporting (ICFR)**.
- Who: Service providers whose systems affect clients’ financial statements (e.g., payroll processors, billing services, data centers, ERP hosts).
- **Purpose**: Gives user companies (and their auditors) assurance that the service provider’s controls are adequate for reliable financial reporting.
- **Types**:
  - Type 1: Design of controls at a point in time.
  - Type 2: Design + operating effectiveness over a period (usually 6–12 months).
- **Key focus**: IT General Controls (access, change management, operations) + application controls tied to financial data.
- **Vs [[SOC 2]]**: SOC 1 = financial reporting only; [[SOC 2]] = security, availability, privacy, etc.
